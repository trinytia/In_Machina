If you have not installed Unity already, get it for free here: https://store.unity.com/#plans-individual
Once everything is installed, open it up and you should be greeted with a project page.
 
STARTING A PROJECT:
>Start a new project by clicking the "New" button at the top right.
>Select the “2D” Template, name your project and then click  to start a new project.

Once inside Unity;
>Drag and drop the PNG version of the file into the “Assets” folder in the   tab, in the bottom left of the main window.
>Click on the Asset icon, in this case “PixelForestPack”, which should open the   on the right-hand side. 

Adjust the following settings: 
>Set “Sprite Mode” from “Single” to “Multiple”
>Set “Pixels per unit” from 100 to 32.

Under “Advanced”;
>Set “Filter Mode” from “Bilinear” to “Point (No filter)”
>Set “Compression” from “Normal Quality” to “None”

Click “Apply”

Within the Inspector;
>Select "Sprite Editor", and “Slice”.
>Change “Type” from “Automatic” to ”Grid By Cell Size”. 
>Set “Pixel Size” X and Y to 32, and then click “Slice”.



Now the Tileset is imported and ready to use. Let us learn how to make maps with them.

In the “Hierarchy” tab on the left;
.Right click on “Main Camera”, then “2D Object” then “Tilemap”. 


On the top row of the Unity Editor;
>click “Window”, “2D”, and then “Tile Palette”. 
  
Drag the tab to the right-hand side to dock it. 

Once in the Tile Palette; 
>click “Create New Palette” and name it what you like, but be sure to remember the name. 
>Click “Select Folder”
>Drag the asset icon into the area marked, and click “Select Folder” again.
>In the Palette Tab select whichever asset you wish to place in the scene.

 

To overlay different tiles, right click “Grid”, then click 2D Object, then Tilemap again. 
 
In  the Tilemap Renderer, set the “Order in Layer” to one higher than the layer you want to be beneath it. Higher ordered numbers are rendered above lower ordered ones.








Your only limit is your own creativity!
